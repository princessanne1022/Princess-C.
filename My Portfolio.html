DC Motor

This project demonstrates a simple DC motor built using basic materials: wooden blocks, strong magnets, a metal rod, wires, and a high-voltage battery. The motor works by converting electrical energy into mechanical motion, showing the principles of electromagnetism in a hands-on and creative way.

Research Project: Automated Plant Fertilizing System and Soil Nutrient Sensor

A capstone project designed to deliver the right amount of nutrients to plants at the right time using sensors and programmed controls. It monitors plant needs, regulates fertilizer flow, and helps maintain healthy growth with minimal human intervention. A soil nutrient sensor measures key nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium directly in the soil. It provides real-time data about soil health, allowing growers to adjust fertilization accurately for optimal plant development.

Research Project: Paper Made Out of Banana Fibers (musa acuminata)

Paper made from banana fibers uses the strong, flexible strands found in the stems of Musa acuminata. These fibers are processed into pulp and formed into sheets, creating an eco-friendly alternative to traditional wood-based paper. The result is durable, biodegradable paper with a natural texture, ideal for crafts, packaging, and sustainable products.

Research Project: Illustration Board Made Out of Sawdust

An illustration board made of sawdust is created by compressing fine wood particles with a binding agent to form a smooth, sturdy surface. This eco-friendly material repurposes wood waste into a durable board ideal for drawing, painting, and other creative projects. Its firm texture provides excellent support for detailed artwork while reducing reliance on traditional paper-based boards.
